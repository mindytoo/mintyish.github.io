SnotBot Using Drones in Whale Research

The Project

In partnership with Ocean Alliance, the Olin Intelligent Vehicles Lab has developed "SnotBot", a specialized drone aimed to follow whales in the ocean and collect samples of whale blow when they surface. SnotBot is to autonomously take off a moving vessel, navigate to a specified target and follow it, hover over and collect blow samples, and return and land on its original vessel.

Field tests with SnotBot White and Grey (custom 3D printed quadcoptors using Pixhawks and operating with ROS and Python) have been conducted at Ocean Alliance headquarters at Gloucester. SnotShot, a small boat acting as a surrogate whale, was used to sense when the drone flew over and shoot artificial whale blow. Additional tests were performed to ensure the amount of noise whales would hear from the drones was not much more distruptive than ambient ocean sounds.

This project was presented at the NERC Robotics Conference in 2014 and is continuing in the Olin Intelligent Vehicles Lab.
