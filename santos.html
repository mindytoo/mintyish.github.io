Santos SCOPE Developing a Capstone Project in Automotive Safety

The Project

Over the summer of 2014, a team of 3 Olin students and I developed the groundwork for a Senior Capstone Program in Engineering (SCOPE) project that joined the interests of a team of Olin seniors, the Santos Family Foundation, and Olin College. However, the Santos Family Foundation was extremely flexible with the direction of the project, provided that the final topic was within the realm of "automotive safety" and Olin college was satisfied knowing there would be some mechanical, robotic, or software aspect, regardless of the proportions of each. Given the huge design space our team had to work with, it was clear that we would have to develop frameworks to keep our exploration concise and our thinking thorough.

The final project has developed a low cost sensor that collects and compiles traffic data to relay to other drivers as well as inform town officials of needed infrastructure improvements.

The Design Process

Adopting the agile working method, we partitioned the 11 weeks into three phases, exploration, conceptualization, and development. Every week would have a some sort of deliverable and all thought processes and group decisions would be documented for handoff to the future SCOPE team. The initial weeks were flooded with potential areas of improvement. Automotive safety was not limited to cars, trucks, and motorcycles but to pedestrians and cyclists. Searching through literature and interviewing commuters, soccer moms, and sports car enthusiasts, we identified numerous comforts and concerns about being on the road.

Eventually, the sheer number of directions that remained by the end of the conceptualize phase lead our team to focus on developing possible projects for the upcoming SCOPE team rather than developing a specific solution for them to embark on fabricating. After collecting and synthesizing data in search of the most effective way to improve automotive safety, we proposed a few projects for the SCOPE team to select from including an interactive windshield display, a verbal car-driver interface, modular safety add-ons, and road user communication kits.
