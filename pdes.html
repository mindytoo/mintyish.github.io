Image Denoising and Inpainting Mumford and Shah Model for Image Processing

The Project

In order to investigate the technicalities and limitations of the Mumford and Shah image processing model, we created a MATLAB model to denoise and inpaint incomplete and corrupted images. The model's algorithm is based on approximations of the Mumford and Shah image processing model and minimizes the diverence between the output image and the expected image. We discuss our adaptation of the algorithm from literature as well as the strengths and weaknesses of the model and implementation.

Introduction

Image processing uses mathematical operations on a visual input, usually photographs or videos, and outputs an altered image or data about the image based on the processing equation and parameters. It has applications in data storage, computer vision, astronomy, and security. The two image processing components we investigate are denoising and inpainting using the Mumford and Shah image processing model.

Denoising is the process of smoothing out an image to remove noise. Noise occurs in images when the value of a particular pixel is incorrect. Salt and pepper noise is when pixels vary in value or intensity from their surroundings. Gaussian noise is when pixels stray from their intended values by a small amount. The frequency of distortion for Gaussian noise is normally distributed.

Inpainting originated from museums and collectors seeking methods to restore art pieces and photographs. Modern computational inpainting uses mathematical algorithms to restore lost or corrupted potions of images. As image processing is an input-output process, the resulting image is highly dependent on both the method of input and the algorithm used to process it. Algorithms intended for restoring highly patterned images, like the leaves of trees, vary considerably from algorithms ntended on restoring more uniform images such as those indoors.

The Mumford and Shah model for image processing manages to both denoise and inpaint its inputs with limitations. Here we investigate image representation and processor modeling by processing small images in MATLAB and reporting our model's results and limitations.
